Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 4, 2026

1. Introduction

ContrastCyber ("we", "us") operates ContrastScan at contrastcyber.com — a free website security scanning tool. This Privacy Policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, and your rights when using the scanner.

Our security intelligence API is a separate property at api.contrastcyber.com with its own Privacy Policy.

2. Data We Collect

2.1 Scan Data

  • Domain names submitted for scanning
  • Scan results (security scores, grades, per-module findings)
  • Passive recon data (DNS records, WHOIS, certificate transparency logs, robots.txt, sitemap entries) — all from public sources

2.2 Technical Data

  • Hashed IP addresses — a one-way keyed HMAC-SHA256 hash of your IP (with a server-side secret as the HMAC key) is stored to associate scans with a stable visitor identifier for rate limiting and abuse detection. The hash is irreversible. No raw IP address is ever stored in the application database.
  • Rate limiting — raw IP addresses are kept transiently in-memory for sliding-window rate limit enforcement and purged automatically.
  • Server logs — standard nginx access logs retained for up to 24 hours (forced daily rotation, rotate 1). The log line contains: IP address, timestamp, HTTP method, request path, status code, response size, referrer URL, and user-agent string.
  • Application logs (systemd journal) — application warning/error events. Retained for 24 hours (MaxRetentionSec=1day in journald.conf).
  • Geographic analysis — a local GeoIP database (MaxMind GeoLite2) maps IP addresses to country-level location only. No city, region, or precise location data is derived. No individual user is identified or profiled.

2.3 What We Do NOT Collect

  • No personal information (name, email, phone) — no signup required, no user accounts
  • No tracking cookies or session cookies
  • No third-party analytics (no Google Analytics, no Facebook Pixel, no fingerprinting)
  • No advertising data
  • We do not sell, rent, or share any data with third parties
  • We do not track users across sites

3. DNT & Global Privacy Control

We respect the Do Not Track (DNT) and Global Privacy Control (Sec-GPC) headers. If your browser sends either signal, we do not store any hashed IP data with your scan. The scanner still works normally — we simply skip the analytics hash.

4. How We Use Data

  • Rate limiting: hashed IP addresses enforce a fair-use limit of 100 requests per hour per IP. Rate limit state is sliding-window and purged automatically.
  • Result lookup: scan records are stored so that you (or any visitor) can re-fetch a result by domain.
  • Analytics: hashed IPs are keyed with a server-side HMAC secret, irreversible, and cannot be traced back to individual users. Used for aggregate counters (total scans/day) only.
  • Geographic analytics: country-level data is used for aggregate traffic analysis. Never linked to individual users or shared externally.

5. Data Retention

  • Scan results: the scan record (domain, grade, findings) is stored indefinitely for historical comparison and result lookup. The user link (hashed client IP column on the scans row) is cleared to an empty string after 90 days via purge_old_client_hashes(), leaving an orphan record that cannot be tied back to any visitor.
  • Rate-limit counters (hashed-IP keyed): sliding 1-hour window — rows older than the current hour are hard-DELETEd by cleanup_ip_limits() / cleanup_api_usage(). No long-term retention.
  • Server logs: rotated and deleted after 24 hours (forced midnight UTC rotation, rotate 1).
  • Application logs: systemd journal capped at 24 hours (MaxRetentionSec=1day).

6. Third-Party Services

We do not sell, rent, or share any data with third parties. The scanner does not integrate with any payment processor, advertising network, or analytics provider. Higher rate limits are sold via our separate API service at api.contrastcyber.com/pricing; payment processing for that service (Lemon Squeezy / NOWPayments) is handled entirely on the API side and never touches the scanner.

7. International Data Transfers

Our server is located in Germany (EU). If you access the scanner from outside the EU, your requests are processed on this EU-based server. We do not transfer data to servers outside the EU.

8. GDPR (EU/EEA Users)

If you are located in the European Union or European Economic Area:

  • Legal basis: we process data based on legitimate interest (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR) — specifically, operating and securing a public security scanner, preventing abuse, and understanding aggregate usage patterns.
  • Data minimization: we collect only what is necessary. IP addresses are hashed with an HMAC server-side secret immediately and never stored in raw form in our database.
  • Your rights: you have the right to access, rectification, erasure, restriction of processing, data portability, and objection. Since we do not maintain user accounts and cannot link hashed data back to individuals, most of these rights are satisfied by design.
  • Right to erasure: contact us at the address below to request removal of any scan record associated with you. We process requests within 30 days.
  • Data Protection Officer: given our small scale and minimal data processing, we have not appointed a DPO. Contact us directly for any privacy concerns.

9. KVKK (Turkish Users)

If you are located in Turkey, your rights under the Personal Data Protection Law (KVKK, Law No. 6698) are respected. You have the right to learn whether your personal data is processed, request information about processing, learn the purpose and whether data is used accordingly, know third parties to whom data is transferred, request correction or deletion, and object to automated processing. Contact us at the address below to exercise these rights.

10. Security

All data is transmitted over HTTPS. The origin uses Cloudflare Authenticated Origin Pulls (mTLS) so direct-origin bypass is rejected. We employ industry-standard server hardening, intrusion detection, file integrity monitoring, fail2ban with custom nginx-blocklist actions, and audit logging. The application is open source and available for public audit.
